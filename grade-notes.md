# Notes Post Grading Prompt

Paste this prompt into Claude (or equivalent) followed by the post content.

---

You are evaluating a short-form note written by a software engineer for a personal site. Notes are not essays. They are not tutorials. They are not hot takes optimized for engagement. They are the thing you write when you have a thought worth keeping — something observed, something reconsidered, something that sharpened after sitting with it.

The target reader is a senior engineer or technical founder. They read quickly and have a high threshold for "this is worth my time." They don't need the thought to be fully developed. They need it to be real.

Grade the post on the following. For each criterion, give a pass, flag, or fail with one sentence of reasoning.

**1. Is this an actual opinion?**
Does the author believe something specific? Or is this restating what most people already think, framed as an observation? Conventional wisdom stated plainly is not a note worth publishing.

**2. Is it short enough?**
A note that runs long is an essay that didn't commit. If it needs more than 300 words to make its point, the point probably needs to be sharpened, not expanded. Flag any section that could be cut without losing the argument.

**3. Does it sound like a person thinking, not a person writing?**
Flag: topic sentences that announce what the paragraph will say, transitions that exist only to connect paragraphs, conclusions that restate the opening. Good notes end when the thought ends, not when the structure demands it.

**4. Would the author be comfortable saying this out loud to a senior engineer they respect?**
If the answer is no — if it's hedged, vague, or crowd-pleasing — it should be revised or cut. The site is not a place for content. It's a place for what the author actually thinks.

**5. Does it overstay its welcome?**
Read the last paragraph. Does it add something, or does it just close the door? If it closes the door, cut it. The reader can find their own way out.

After grading each criterion, give an overall verdict: **publish**, **revise**, or **cut**. If revise, name the one sentence that is doing the most damage.
